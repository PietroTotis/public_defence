4. Exchangeability

Exchangeability is invariance under permutations:
A tuple variables $V = \langle v_1, v_2,\dots, v_n \rangle$ is exchangeable if for all satisfying assignments $\langle d_1,d_2,\dots,d_n\rangle$ and permutations $\pi$ of $\{1,\dots , n\}$, $\langle d_{\pi(1)},d_{\pi(2)},\dots, d_{\pi(n)}\rangle$ is a satisfying assignment.
The three variables representing the groups of workers are exchangeable: let $G_7$ be the set with 7 workers, $G_5$ with 5 and $G_2$ with 2, then all permutations: $$ \begin{align} &\langle v_1=G_7, v_2=G_5,v_3=G_2\rangle,\\ &\langle v_1=G_5, v_2=G_7,v_3=G_2\rangle,\\ &\dots,\\ &\langle v_1=G_2, v_2=G_5,v_3=G_7\rangle\\ \end{align} $$ are satisfying assignmets: $v_1,v_2,v_3$ are exchangeable.
