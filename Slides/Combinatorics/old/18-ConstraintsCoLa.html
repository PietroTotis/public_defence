3. Constraints

  • Positional constraints - specify the object or multiset in a specific position.
    universe people = {ann, bob, carl, dan};
    queue in [people]; #queue = 4;
    queue[1] in {ann, dan};
Towards reasoning:
  • Size constraints allow us to reason over cardinality of sets without looking at individual objects
  • Positional constraints break symmetries in the assignment of variables
  • Counting constraints present a symmetry in the assignment of variables
