3. Constraints - First order logic

Consider counting constraints, e.g. "At least two TVs are defective". In FOL we have to explicitly write down the combinations of variables that satisfy the count. Let $tv_1,\dots,tv_5$ be the variables representing the subset of purchased TVs. To specify that exactly two of the purchased TVs are defective we constrain the domain of the variables: $$ \mathit{df}(tv_1)\land \mathit{df}(tv_2) \land \lnot\mathit{df}(tv_3)\land \lnot \mathit{df}(tv_4)\land \lnot \mathit{df}(tv_5))\lor $$ $$ \mathit{df}(tv_1)\land \lnot\mathit{df}(tv_2) \land \mathit{df}(tv_3)\lnot \mathit{df}(tv_4)\land \lnot \mathit{df}(tv_5)) $$ $$ \lor \dots\lor $$ $$ \lnot \mathit{df}(tv_1)\land \lnot \mathit{df}(tv_2) \land \lnot\mathit{df}(tv_3)\land \mathit{df}(tv_4)\land \mathit{df}(tv_5) $$
