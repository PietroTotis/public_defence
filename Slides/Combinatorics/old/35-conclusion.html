Takeaways

  1. A two-steps approach combines the state-of-the-art performances of neural networks on natural language understanding with the generalizability, explainability and modularity of declarative languages and solvers.
  2. The design of the formal language is fundamental to cover the domain of interest.
  3. There is however a trade-off between the expressivity of the language and the complexity/efficiency of the solver.
  4. Lifted reasoning can speed-up exponentially the solving times for problems with symmetries and aggregate operations.
